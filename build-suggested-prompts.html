Suggested Prompts

Templates for working with ADA, adaptable to any assessment design scenario

The specific prompts will vary depending on your context and assessment challenge. Below are general structures that work well as starting points. Adapt the bracketed sections to your situation. Use the Copy button on each block to copy the template to your clipboard.

Opening prompt: setting the scene

Prompt templateI am a course leader for [course name/description] at [level]. As part of this course, I set [assessment type] that requires students to [brief description of the task]. My concern is [describe the specific challenge or opportunity]. I attach [document name]. Review this document thoroughly, summarise your understanding of the project, the expectations, and the marking criteria. Then, using this as a starting point, give me options for [what you want to explore].

Exploring options

Prompt templateI would go for [option]. I like the idea of [specific element]. I also like [another element]. Can you help me structure this approach?

Requesting implementation details

Prompt templateCan you show me the full [marking criteria / assessment brief / feedback template] taking into account all of the decisions we have made? Also, [any additional considerations, for example "remove all criteria related to presentation" or "add a section on student choice"].

Challenging ADA’s suggestions

Prompt templateI am not sure I understand what [concept] means in this context. Can you help me understand it so I can decide whether to keep it or not?

Requesting the decision pathway

Prompt templateCan you review our conversation and draw the decision pathway we went through together?
